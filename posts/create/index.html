companies worth creating

August 8, 2023

George Hotz had a great talk at Comma Con in which he said:

Build companies that will change the future, not the distribution of wealth in the future.

That’s a nice line. Some products are in crowded spaces that were just a matter of time - the nth frontend authentication product might be one. These will materially change the distribution of wealth in the future but not the future itself. On the other hand, it’s clear that there exist companies that change the future - Apple changed the future, OpenAI is changing the future.

Scott Alexander makes a related point here in his piece on replaceability and billionaires, where he discusses various levels of replaceability in entrepreneurship. Amazon could have existed a few years later without Bezos, but SpaceX probably wouldn’t have happened with Musk. And the point he ends with is that the value that you create scales with how materially you positively change the future.

This story says nothing about value capture - companies are vehicles of capital, after all - where profit is the dominant metric. But value capture is upper bounded by creation. And so a helpful condition for a successful company is that the product should change the future.
