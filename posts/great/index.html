Greatness

December 28, 2023

“Greatness is never simply a multiplier of the ordinary; it has other qualities entirely.”

andy grove#

Andy grove was an extremely tough pragmatist. He worked incredibly hard, asked cutting questions, while at the same time treating people with respect.

max levchin#

He is extremely intelligent and hard working. He was obviously a better programmer than anyone else when he started paypal. Lacked strong principles and some business/product sense at that time.

sam altman#

Sam Altman is very ambitious, a clear thinker, and good with people. His problem solving ability in the real world is great. He doesn’t care what people think about him. His flaw is that he rubs people the wrong way with the ambition sometimes and is a bit all over the place.

qasar younis#

Qasar is a radical pragmatist. He has an ability to understand markets, work extremely hard, and be an energetic and aggressive sales person. His energy and storytelling make him very memorable. In most economic transactions he has made, both people have come out ahead it seems.

richard feynman#

Richard Feynman loves puzzles. He follows his curiosity relentlessly. He has a strong set of values and is very honest. He allows himself to do things that bother other people so long as they don’t hurt his internal value system.

ilya sutskever#

Ilya is a man of conviction. He boils down ideas into the bare minimum and allows himself to make optimistic speculations on the future.

brian chesky#

elon musk#

Elon has this fearlessness, strong set of internal values, and relentless execution ability. He is also extremely technical and follows his nose on problems well. He is obsessive about product.

paul graham#

Paul really respects the truth. He is all substance, very determined, but sensitive to situations. He also has developed great taste.

parker conrad#

Parker’s story is a case study that paranoia is good actually. The chip on your shoulder you get from realizing that it can all drop from under you at any minute is not replaceable. Parker is relentless – I would rather wrestle a grizzly than compete with him.

peter thiel#

Peter thiel has designed himself to be a generative thinker on technology, economics, and on human nature. He is very creative and understands the real (as opposed to the purported) nature and purpose of things. He has a debater’s communication style and is quite incisive. He understands and respects outliers since they cause technological change.
