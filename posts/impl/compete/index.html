Compete

May 20, 2023

“I’d rather wrestle grizzlies than compete with Mrs. B.” - Warren Buffett

Some people are so good that you know they will do something great. Here we discuss the components of this in a famously good founder - Rose Blumkin. We’ll go through some of her exemplary qualities and go through some evidence. Much of this is take taken from the excellent Founder’s podcast on the subject.

Intrinsic drive + determination

  • Business slowed down after the korean war. After taking a 50k loan for 90 days, held a three-day sale and earned 250k. No loans again.
  • Half of her store was destroyed in a fire. Massive discounts on stuff with smoke damage - sold a lot of stuff.
  • Mrs. B worked until she was 104. Was relentless and never took time off.
  • Very decisive and gut oriented. Doesn’t look back.

Obsessed with customer

  • The algorithm is to understand what customers want and find a way to give it to them.
  • Furniture customers want low prices. So Blumkin’s approach was to buy cheaply and pass on to customers most of the savings.
    • Best businesses have exceptional value to customers, which translates to exceptional economics for their owners.
  • Blumkin’s favorite thing to do in the evening is to just talk to customers at the store

Competitive

  • “Mr. Buffett, we’re going to put our competitors through a meat grinder” - Blumkin
  • No one would compete on furniture in Omaha because Mrs. B was so fearsome.
  • Favorite thing to do on a weekend - go to a competitor’s store and plan her next attack.
  • One mental model: if I had ample capital and the right personnel, could I compete? Buffett: Hell no.

No Complacency

  • Hang up “going out of business” from competitors to show the existential risk
  • Immigrant mentality adds to this - Blumkin had a chip on her shoulder.

A question to you is: would people rather wrestle a grizzly than compete with you in your field?
